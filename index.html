Establishment

Buenos Aires was founded in 1536 (according to other sources in 1535) by the Spanish conquistador Pedro Mendoza[8] on the southern shore of La Plata and included a large settlement, which was later used as a base for the movement of settlers inland, and a convenient harbor that provided opportunity to travel across the Atlantic. The city was named Real de Nuestra Señora Santa María del Buen Aire (Spanish: Real de Nuestra Señora Santa María del Buen Aire) in honor of Our Lady of Bonaria, the patron saint of sailors.

Natural conditions

The capital is located in the southeastern part of Argentina, on a flat area, in a subtropical natural zone. The natural vegetation of the city's outskirts is represented by tree and grass species typical of meadow steppes and savannas.

Economy

In 2008, the city's GDP was 400.5 billion pesos[31]. The main sector of the city's economy is the service sector, which represents 78% of the GDP of Buenos Aires and 56% of the country. One of the most dynamically developing industries is construction.
